# Operational Vocabulary


# Purpose

Operational Vocabulary standardizes the practical terms used to describe real-world operations within GPS OS.

These terms provide a consistent language for documenting operational knowledge, analyzing workflows, and designing decision systems.

A shared operational vocabulary reduces ambiguity between domain experts, software developers, and AI.

---

# Standard Vocabulary

## Operation

A sequence of activities performed to achieve a business objective.

Operations exist in the real world before they are represented in software.

---

## Workflow

The ordered flow of activities, decisions, and information within an operation.

Workflows describe how work actually happens.

---

## Process

A structured and repeatable workflow designed to achieve consistent outcomes.

Processes are standardized implementations of operational knowledge.

---

## Task

The smallest executable unit of work within a workflow.

Tasks are performed by people, systems, or AI.

---

## Decision Point

A moment within a workflow where one or more alternative actions must be evaluated.

Decision points should be supported by explicit reasoning rather than assumptions.

---

## Constraint

A condition or limitation that influences operational decisions.

Constraints may include regulations, business rules, resources, time, technology, or organizational policies.

---

## Exception

A situation that falls outside the normal operational flow and requires additional reasoning or alternative handling.

Exceptions should be identified explicitly rather than hidden within standard workflows.

---

## Outcome

The observable result produced by completing an operation or workflow.

Outcomes provide evidence for evaluating whether decisions and implementations are effective.

---

# Principle

Operational vocabulary should describe reality as it exists.

It should remain independent of specific software implementations, technologies, or programming languages.
