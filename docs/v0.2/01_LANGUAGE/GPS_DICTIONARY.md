# GPS Dictionary


# Purpose

The GPS Dictionary defines the canonical vocabulary used throughout GPS OS.

Each term has one intended meaning.

Humans and AI should interpret these terms consistently across documentation, models, methods, applications, and implementations.

When a term is defined here, that definition becomes the canonical reference for GPS OS.

---

# Canonical Terms

## Reality

The actual operational environment, including people, processes, constraints, events, and observable facts.

Reality is the starting point of every GPS OS initiative.

---

## Operational Knowledge

Knowledge gained through performing real work, solving practical problems, and understanding how operations function in practice.

Operational knowledge is the primary source of reasoning within GPS OS.

---

## Shared Reasoning Language

A common language that enables humans and AI to understand operational reality, reason consistently, and make aligned decisions.

It is the core identity of GPS OS.

---

## Reasoning

The process of transforming observations and knowledge into understanding that supports consistent decisions.

Reasoning connects reality to action.

---

## Decision

A justified conclusion reached through shared reasoning.

Decisions guide implementation.

They should not depend on individual interpretation alone.

---

## Software

The implementation of validated decisions into reliable, repeatable systems.

Software is an outcome of reasoning, not its starting point.

---

## GPS Kernel

The fundamental reasoning flow of GPS OS:

Reality

↓

Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Software

Every model, method, and application should preserve this sequence.
