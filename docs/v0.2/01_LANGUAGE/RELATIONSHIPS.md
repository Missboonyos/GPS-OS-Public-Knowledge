# Relationships


# Purpose

Relationships define how the core concepts of GPS OS connect to form a coherent reasoning system.

Individual concepts have limited value when viewed in isolation.

Understanding their relationships enables humans and AI to reason consistently across different contexts and applications.

---

# Primary Relationship

The GPS Kernel defines the primary relationship within GPS OS.

Reality

↓

Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Software

Each stage depends on the integrity of the previous stage.

---

# Supporting Relationships

## Reality → Operational Knowledge

Operational knowledge is derived from observing and understanding reality.

Without reality, operational knowledge cannot be validated.

---

## Operational Knowledge → Shared Reasoning

Operational knowledge provides the evidence used in shared reasoning.

Reasoning should always be grounded in practical experience and observable facts.

---

## Shared Language → Shared Reasoning

Shared reasoning depends on a shared language.

Consistent terminology reduces ambiguity and enables humans and AI to interpret concepts in the same way.

---

## Shared Reasoning → Decision

Reasoning transforms knowledge into justified decisions.

Reliable decisions should be explainable and traceable.

---

## Decision → Software

Software implements decisions.

Changes to software should originate from validated changes in reasoning or operational reality.

---

# Design Principle

Relationships are directional.

Each concept builds upon another.

GPS OS avoids circular reasoning by preserving a clear flow from operational reality to software implementation.
