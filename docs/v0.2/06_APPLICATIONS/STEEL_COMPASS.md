# Steel Compass



# Purpose

Steel Compass demonstrates the application of GPS OS to a real-world operational domain.

It serves as the first implementation of the GPS Method, showing how operational knowledge can be transformed into reliable decision-support software.

The objective is not only to build a software product, but also to validate that the principles and methodology of GPS OS are effective in practice.

---

# Operational Domain

Steel Compass focuses on import compliance for steel products.

Importers must navigate multiple sources of regulatory information, including trade remedies, product standards, and import requirements.

These requirements are often distributed across different organizations and presented in ways that make consistent decision-making difficult.

---

# Operational Problem

Import compliance decisions require combining information from multiple operational sources.

Manual interpretation increases the risk of:

- Inconsistent decisions
- Human error
- Missed regulatory requirements
- Repeated research effort

The challenge is not the lack of information, but the difficulty of organizing and applying it consistently.

---

# GPS OS Application

Steel Compass applies the GPS Method by:

- Observing real operational workflows
- Modeling operational decisions
- Structuring domain knowledge
- Designing explicit decision logic
- Building software that preserves operational reasoning
- Validating results against operational reality

The product demonstrates how operational knowledge can become reusable software without losing its original intent.

---

# Outcomes

Steel Compass demonstrates that GPS OS can:

- Transform operational expertise into software
- Preserve reasoning within system design
- Improve consistency of operational decisions
- Reduce repetitive manual analysis
- Create reusable operational knowledge

These outcomes validate the practical application of GPS OS in a complex operational environment.
