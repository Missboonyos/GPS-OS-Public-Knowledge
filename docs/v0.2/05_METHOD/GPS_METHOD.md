# GPS Method



# Purpose

The GPS Method defines the end-to-end process used by GPS OS to transform operational knowledge into reliable software.

It provides a repeatable methodology that preserves reality, reasoning, validation, and knowledge throughout product development.

The objective is not simply to build software, but to build software that faithfully represents operational understanding.

---

# Principle

Understand reality before designing solutions.

Every implementation should be traceable back to validated operational knowledge.

The GPS Method emphasizes understanding before automation, reasoning before implementation, and validation before standardization.

---

# The GPS Method

Every product developed with GPS OS follows the same operational sequence:

Discover Reality

↓

Understand the Problem

↓

Model the Operation

↓

Reason and Decide

↓

Design the Architecture

↓

Create the Specification

↓

Build the Software

↓

Validate Against Reality

↓

Capture Knowledge

↓

Improve Continuously

This sequence creates a continuous learning cycle that strengthens both future products and future decisions.

---

# Method Characteristics

The GPS Method is:

- Reality-driven
- Knowledge-centered
- Evidence-based
- Explainable
- Traceable
- Repeatable
- Technology-independent

The method remains stable even when tools, programming languages, frameworks, or AI models evolve.

---

# Expected Outcomes

Applying the GPS Method should produce:

- Shared understanding between humans and AI
- Reliable operational software
- Reusable knowledge assets
- Transparent decision making
- Continuous organizational learning

These outcomes enable products to evolve without losing their operational foundations.
