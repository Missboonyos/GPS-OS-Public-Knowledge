# Product Blueprint



# Purpose

The Product Blueprint defines the standard structure for designing products with GPS OS.

Rather than starting from features or technology, every product begins with operational understanding and progresses through a consistent design process.

The objective is to ensure that products preserve operational intent while remaining adaptable to different domains and implementation technologies.

---

# Principle

Products should be designed from reality outward.

Technology serves the product.

The product serves operational needs.

Operational reality remains the foundation.

---

# Blueprint Structure

Every product should define the following elements:

- Product Vision
- Operational Context
- Problem Statement
- Target Users
- Desired Outcomes
- Operational Workflow
- Decision Model
- Knowledge Requirements
- System Architecture
- Functional Scope
- Validation Strategy
- Success Criteria

Together, these elements create a complete operational blueprint before implementation begins.

---

# Product Design Flow

Every product follows the same high-level design sequence:

Understand Reality

↓

Define the Problem

↓

Identify Users

↓

Design the Workflow

↓

Define Decisions

↓

Design Knowledge Structure

↓

Design Architecture

↓

Specify Requirements

↓

Build

↓

Validate

↓

Learn

This sequence ensures that implementation remains aligned with validated operational understanding.

---

# Blueprint Characteristics

A Product Blueprint should be:

- Reality-driven
- User-centered
- Knowledge-oriented
- Explainable
- Traceable
- Technology-independent

The blueprint documents operational intent rather than implementation details.
