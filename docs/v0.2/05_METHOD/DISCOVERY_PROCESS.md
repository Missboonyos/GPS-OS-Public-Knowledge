# Discovery Process



# Purpose

The Discovery Process defines how GPS OS investigates operational reality before designing solutions.

Rather than beginning with assumptions, features, or technology, discovery starts by understanding how work actually happens, where problems occur, and why they occur.

The objective is to establish a shared understanding of reality that can serve as the foundation for reliable software.

---

# Principle

Discovery precedes design.

No architecture, specification, or implementation should begin before operational reality has been sufficiently understood.

Good discovery reduces uncertainty and improves the quality of every decision that follows.

---

# Discovery Process

Every discovery effort follows the same sequence:

Observe Reality

↓

Identify Stakeholders

↓

Understand Operational Context

↓

Map the Workflow

↓

Identify Problems

↓

Identify Decisions

↓

Identify Knowledge

↓

Validate Understanding

↓

Define the Operational Model

↓

Begin Product Design

Each step strengthens the shared understanding required before implementation begins.

---

# Discovery Outputs

A successful discovery process should produce:

- Operational Context
- Stakeholder Understanding
- Workflow Model
- Problem Model
- Decision Model
- Knowledge Model
- Initial Product Scope
- Validation Evidence

These outputs become the foundation for subsequent architecture, specification, and implementation.

---

# Discovery Characteristics

The Discovery Process should be:

- Reality-driven
- Evidence-based
- Collaborative
- Explainable
- Traceable
- Iterative

Discovery is not a one-time activity.

It continues whenever new operational knowledge emerges.

---
