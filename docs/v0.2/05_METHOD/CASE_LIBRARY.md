# Case Library


# Purpose

The Case Library defines how GPS OS captures real-world operational cases as reusable knowledge.

While patterns describe recurring structures, cases preserve the complete context of individual situations.

The objective is to build an evidence-based library of operational experience that both humans and AI can study, compare, and learn from.

---

# Principle

Reality is the richest source of knowledge.

Every validated case contributes to a deeper understanding of operational complexity and strengthens future reasoning.

Cases preserve experience before it is generalized into patterns.

---

# Components of a Case

Every case should identify the following elements:

- Case Identifier
- Title
- Operational Context
- Background
- Problem
- Constraints
- Evidence
- Reasoning Process
- Decision
- Outcome
- Lessons Learned
- Related Patterns

Together, these elements preserve the complete reasoning journey from reality to outcome.

---

# Case Lifecycle

Every case follows the same lifecycle:

Capture

↓

↓

Validate

↓

Classify

↓

Store

↓

Discover

↓

Compare

↓

Learn

The library grows continuously as new operational experience becomes available.

---

# Case Representation

Each case should clearly distinguish between:

- Facts
- Assumptions
- Evidence
- Reasoning
- Decisions
- Outcomes
- Lessons Learned

This structure allows humans and AI to understand not only what happened, but why it happened and what can be learned from it.
