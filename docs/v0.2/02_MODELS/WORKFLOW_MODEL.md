# Workflow Model


# Purpose

The Workflow Model defines how GPS OS represents the flow of work within an operational system.

Workflows describe how activities, information, decisions, and responsibilities interact to produce operational outcomes.

The objective is to understand how work actually happens before redesigning or automating it.

---

# Principle

A workflow represents reality in motion.

Rather than documenting isolated tasks, GPS OS models the complete flow of work from initiation to completion.

Understanding the workflow reveals dependencies, bottlenecks, risks, and opportunities for improvement.

---

# Components of a Workflow

Every workflow should identify the following elements:

- Trigger
- Activities
- Decision Points
- Information Flow
- Roles and Responsibilities
- Constraints
- Exceptions
- Outcomes

Together, these elements provide a complete representation of operational execution.

---

# Workflow Analysis

Workflow analysis seeks to answer questions such as:

- How does work begin?
- What sequence of activities occurs?
- Where are decisions made?
- What information is required?
- What constraints influence the flow?
- Where do exceptions occur?
- How is success measured?

The objective is to understand the operational logic rather than simply document procedures.

---

# Workflow Representation

A workflow should be represented as a sequence of observable operational events.

The representation should distinguish between:

- Standard flow
- Alternative flow
- Exception flow
- Decision points
- Information exchanges

This structure enables humans and AI to analyze workflows consistently.
