# Decision Model


# Purpose

The Decision Model defines how GPS OS represents and evaluates operational decisions.

A decision is not an isolated choice.

It is the result of structured reasoning based on operational reality, available knowledge, defined constraints, and intended outcomes.

The objective is to make decision-making transparent, explainable, and repeatable for both humans and AI.

---

# Principle

Reliable decisions are built on reliable reasoning.

GPS OS treats every important decision as a traceable reasoning process rather than an intuitive judgment.

---

# Components of a Decision

Every decision should identify the following elements:

- Context
- Objective
- Available Options
- Decision Criteria
- Supporting Evidence
- Constraints
- Trade-offs
- Decision
- Expected Outcome

Together, these elements create a complete representation of the reasoning behind a decision.

---

# Decision Analysis

Decision analysis seeks to answer questions such as:

- What decision needs to be made?
- Why is this decision necessary?
- What evidence supports each option?
- What constraints influence the decision?
- What trade-offs exist?
- What risks accompany each option?
- What outcome is expected?

The objective is to ensure that decisions can be explained, reviewed, and improved over time.

---

# Decision Representation

A decision should clearly distinguish between:

- Facts
- Assumptions
- Evidence
- Alternatives
- Trade-offs
- Final Decision

This structure enables humans and AI to reach consistent conclusions using the same reasoning process.

---

# Relationship to GPS Kernel

The Decision Model represents the transition from reasoning to implementation.
