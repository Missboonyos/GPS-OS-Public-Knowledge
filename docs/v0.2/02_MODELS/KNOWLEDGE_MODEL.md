# Knowledge Model


# Purpose

The Knowledge Model defines how GPS OS captures, organizes, and reuses operational knowledge.

Knowledge is more than information.

It is validated understanding derived from operational reality, structured reasoning, and accumulated experience.

The objective is to transform individual expertise into reusable knowledge that can be consistently understood by both humans and AI.

---

# Principle

Knowledge should be reusable.

GPS OS treats knowledge as an evolving organizational asset rather than isolated documentation.

Knowledge should preserve the reasoning behind decisions, not only their outcomes.

---

# Components of Knowledge

Every knowledge object should identify the following elements:

- Context
- Operational Reality
- Problem
- Reasoning
- Decision
- Outcome
- Lessons Learned
- Reuse Potential

Together, these elements preserve both knowledge and the logic that produced it.

---

# Knowledge Organization

Knowledge should be organized around operational understanding rather than document types.

Examples include:

- Decision patterns
- Workflow patterns
- Business rules
- Operational constraints
- Case studies
- Best practices
- Lessons learned

This organization enables knowledge to be discovered and reused across different domains.

---

# Knowledge Representation

Knowledge should distinguish between:

- Facts
- Observations
- Reasoning
- Decisions
- Evidence
- Reusable Patterns

This structure allows humans and AI to understand not only what happened, but why it happened.

---
