# Reality Model


# Purpose

The Reality Model defines how GPS OS observes and represents the real operational world.

Every software product begins with reality, not assumptions.

The purpose of this model is to ensure that humans and AI establish a shared understanding of the operational environment before identifying problems or proposing solutions.

---

# Principle

Reality is the source of truth.

Reality exists independently of software, documentation, or human interpretation.

GPS OS treats reality as something to be observed, understood, and validated before it is modeled.

---

# Components of Reality

Operational reality consists of interconnected elements:

- People
- Activities
- Information
- Decisions
- Constraints
- Events
- Outcomes

These elements form the context in which work actually happens.

---

# Observing Reality

Understanding reality requires evidence rather than assumptions.

Evidence may include:

- Direct observation
- Operational documents
- Business rules
- Regulations
- Historical records
- Interviews with domain experts
- Measurable operational data

Multiple sources should be combined to create a complete understanding.

---

# Representation

The Reality Model does not simplify reality by removing important context.

Instead, it organizes observable facts into a structured representation that supports consistent reasoning.

This representation becomes the foundation for all subsequent models.

---

# Relationship to GPS Kernel

The Reality Model corresponds to the first stage of the GPS Kernel.

Reality

↓

Reasoning

↓

Decision

↓

Software

Every subsequent model depends on an accurate understanding of reality.

If reality is misunderstood, every downstream decision becomes less reliable.
